# Mission

## Why am I learning this?

I work at a Chinese used-car transaction company that is preparing to go public. We need to monitor the dynamics of already-listed companies in the same space on the US market, as input to our strategic investment decisions — understanding competitors' financial condition, strategic moves, and risk signals, so we can calibrate our own IPO timing and business strategy.

I need to master **the domain knowledge behind this monitoring system** (not the coding implementation), to the point that I can explain to my colleagues in the Strategic Investment department what every piece of the system does, why it is designed the way it is, and what a given result means.

## What will I be able to do?

- Read an SEC filing: understand its type, structure, and key information, and know where to find what
- Understand the core logic of competitor monitoring in the used-car space: who to monitor, which metrics to watch, and why
- When handed a dashboard output or briefing from the system, judge "is this piece of information important, does it need to be escalated"
- In a Strategic Investment department meeting, explain how the monitoring system works and the value of its output

## What won't I learn?

- Python programming, Streamlit development, API calls — already know them
- LLM prompt engineering — let the coding agent handle it
- SEC legal and compliance details — this is not a law course

## Success criteria

- Can independently explain to a colleague who "doesn't know SEC" what 10-K, 10-Q, and 8-K are and why all three matter
- Can explain which 5 competitors we selected, why these 5, and what to monitor most closely at each
- Sees an 8-K alert (e.g., "Item 5.02 executive departure") and knows how serious it is without asking anyone
- Can read an earnings call briefing and the key data in a 10-Q and form an independent judgment within 5 minutes
