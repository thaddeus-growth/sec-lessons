# Resources

## Primary (highest trust, start here)

### SEC official
- [SEC EDGAR — How to Research Public Companies](https://www.sec.gov/edgar/about) — SEC's official EDGAR primer
- [SEC Beginners Guide to Financial Statements](https://www.sec.gov/about/reports-publications/investor-publications/beginners-guide-financial-statements) — SEC's introduction to reading financial statements (for non-financial readers)
- [EDGAR Form Types](https://www.sec.gov/files/forms-listed.pdf) — Complete list of all SEC form types
- [How to Read a 10-K](https://www.sec.gov/files/reada10k.pdf) — SEC's official guide to reading a 10-K
- [How to Read an 8-K](https://www.sec.gov/files/reada8k.pdf) — SEC's official guide to reading an 8-K

### Strategic investment / competitor analysis
- [CFI — Competitor Analysis Framework](https://corporatefinanceinstitute.com/resources/management/competitor-analysis/) — Classic framework for competitor analysis
- [Aswath Damodaran — Valuation and financial statement analysis](https://pages.stern.nyu.edu/~adamodar/) — Wall Street's most famous valuation professor; all course materials free
- [Investopedia — Introduction to financial statement analysis](https://www.investopedia.com/articles/basics/06/financialreporting.asp) — More accessible than the SEC's official guide

### Used-car industry specific
- [Carvana Investor Relations](https://investors.carvana.com/) — Go directly to Carvana's IR page to see how a listed company communicates with investors
- [NADA — Auto Retail Industry Data](https://www.nada.org/nada/industry-analysis) — Industry data from the National Automobile Dealers Association

## Secondary (useful depth)

- [XBRL US — What is XBRL?](https://xbrl.us/home/consumers/what-is-xbrl/) — Explains what XBRL is (the machine-readable format for financial statements)
- [Seeking Alpha — Financial statement interpretation articles](https://seekingalpha.com/) — See how professional analysts interpret financial statements (note: opinions may be biased)
- [Fool.com — Earnings Call Transcripts](https://www.fool.com/earnings-call-transcripts/) — Free source of EC transcripts

## Reference (consult while working)

- [edgartools documentation](https://edgartools.readthedocs.io/) — The Python library we use; understand its capability boundary
- [SEC EDGAR API documentation](https://www.sec.gov/edgar/sec-api-documentation) — The capability boundary of the underlying API

## Cross-holding data sources (new — Lessons 0006-0009)

### 13F institutional holdings (Lesson 0006)
- [WhaleWisdom](https://whalewisdom.com/) — 13F aggregation + 13D/G tracking; the free tier shows top institutional holders. Use it to validate our 13F collection results
- [Dataroma](https://www.dataroma.com/) — Visualization of superinvestors' 13F position changes. Free, see real data and learn how "one institution holding multiple stocks" is presented
- [13F video tutorial — Everything Money](https://www.youtube.com/results?search_query=how+to+use+13f+filings) — Explains what 13F can and cannot do, and what the 45-day lag means (~12 min)

### Form 4 insider trading (Lesson 0007)
- [OpenInsider](http://openinsider.com/) — The best insider-trading data site, free. Search CVNA/KMX/AN directly to see what real Form 4 data looks like
- [Form 4 video — Investor's Business Daily](https://www.youtube.com/results?search_query=form+4+insider+trading) — Basic Form 4 walkthrough (~8 min)

### 13D/G Activists (Lesson 0008)
- [13D Monitor](https://13dmonitor.com/) — Published by Schulte Roth & Zabel; the industry's most professional activist-tracking newsletter
- [Aswath Damodaran — Activist Investing](https://pages.stern.nyu.edu/~adamodar/) — NYU Stern valuation professor's public lecture on when and why activists get involved (~20 min)
